Change control in a construction project

The management of change control in a construction project is crucial to ensure that the primary objectives of time, cost, and quality are achieved with minimal deviation. This process involves detecting, evaluating, approving, and implementing modifications that may arise during project execution.

During my experience as a construction manager, I have been able to verify that an effective change control system can mean the difference between the success and failure of a construction project.

Change Control Phases

1

Identification

Detect the need for change through continuous site supervision and communication with all stakeholders involved.

2

Evaluation

Analyze the impact of the change on project time, cost, quality and resources through detailed technical studies.

3

Approval

Obtain the necessary authorization from project managers and clients before proceeding with implementation.

4

Implementation

Execute the change in a controlled manner, documenting the entire process and ensuring the quality of the final result.

Case Study: Structural Modification

In a recent residential project, there was a need to modify the structure of a floor to adapt it to new client requirements. Thanks to a clear change control protocol, we were able to:

  • Evaluate the structural impact within 48 hours
  • Obtain approval from the client and structural engineer
  • Implement changes without affecting the overall schedule
  • Maintain the initial budget with an increase of only 3%
